I am an international lawyer focusing on the varieties of displacement that arise in the context of the triple planetary crisis. As people are forced to leave their homes due to changing weather patterns, environmental pollution and biodiversity loss, law shapes who is displaced, how they experience being displaced, and when or whether they will find a durable solution. 

Following doctoral research on how international refugee law applies to people forced to cross international borders in the context of disasters and climate change, since 2018 I have led the work of the Raoul Wallenberg Institute of Human Rights and Humanitarian Law as it has sought to advance understanding of and respect for the human rights of people exposed to internal displacement risk. Starting with a regional initiative across Asia and the Pacific, I have also worked with colleagues and partners in academia, local and national government, and international organisations around the world. Much of this practical work has involved translating international human rights standards and guidelines into practical disaster risk reduction and climate change adaptation settings. Through many collaborations, I have pioneered the Framework for Integrating Human Rights and Gender Equality (FIRE), which helps non-lawyers to systematically address human rights and gender equality dimensions of their work.

Starting in 2026, my primary focus is on examining how justice sector actors respond to legal claims concerning urban environmental displacement. This research, supported by the Swedish Sustainability Research Council (Formas), synthesises insights from judicial decisions concerning failures to prevent displacement, protect people during displacement, and facilitate durable solutions to displacement. It is informed by urban feminist political ecology and uses a transnational legal pluralist perspective to examine how overlapping international and domestic legal frameworks guide judicial and quasi-judicial decisions. This research is part of a wider collaboration between the Raoul Wallenberg Institute, the Global Strategic Litigation Council and Earth Refuge centering on the Climate Mobility Case Database. The CMC Database aims to provide a comprehensive resource for the 650+ member organisations of the Council, as well as other legal practitioners and academics, focusing on both cross-border and internal displacement in the context of the triple planetary crisis.
